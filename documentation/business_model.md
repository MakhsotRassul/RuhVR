Commercialization analysis and business model architecture of RuhVR

Problem, solution, and value proposition:
Low level of student engagement in the process of studying history, lack of visual three-dimensional teaching aids in educational institutions, shortage of interactive exhibits in regional museums.
The solution is a hardware and software complex RuhVR. The avatar robot performs the function of attracting attention and serving as a physical interface. The VR platform provides deep immersion into the historical context. AR cards provide mobile access to 3D models of artifacts.
Value proposition: An integrated ecosystem of a digital teacher, combining anthropomorphic robotics and immersive software with the preservation of national identity.

Market segmentation and distribution channels in the B2B segment:

General education schools and innovative lyceums: Supply of AR card kits and subscription to the RuhVR VR platform for conducting history laboratory and lecture classes.

Museum complexes and exhibitions: Sale or long-term lease of RuhVR humanoid robots customized for the excursion routes of a specific museum.

Centers of additional education: Using the robot as an interactive teacher's assistant to demonstrate the connection between engineering and culture.

Project economics and scalability:
Revenues are generated through direct sales of robotic platforms, licensing fees for the use of VR and AR software, as well as customization of content to customer requirements.
Scalability: The software part is updated remotely via a website. The robot's design is based on the modular principle of pib and InMoov modification, which allows adapting the appearance to the cultural code of any region or country.

Alignment with global UN goals:
Goal 4, Quality education: Implementation of advanced visual and robotic technologies into the educational process to increase the accessibility of knowledge.
Goal 11, Sustainable cities and communities: Ensuring the preservation of the world cultural heritage by creating accurate digital twins of vulnerable historical sites.
